Patterns of Energy Production and Consumption within Europe and within the Republic of Ireland

A project by Drew Dollison, Jayden Rogers, and Gretchen Klock

.

Goals and Objectives

We aim to visualize patterns of energy production, and consumption in Ireland as well as flows of natural gas in Europe. This includes studying issues of access to electricity (including affordability) and the security of electricity.

Objective: We plan to identify changes in Ireland energy production and use over time. Additionally, we will identify patterns for energy used for central heating which is another major source of energy use within the country.

Through mapping energy sources, we will also be able to visualize the balance of domestic energy sources (peat coal, natural gas, and hydroelectric, later wind) and imported energy sources (oil, natural gas, coal).

We will frame our analysis within the context of the European natural gas trade. This is an especially relevant topic due to recent disruptions and shortages caused by the war in Ukraine.
